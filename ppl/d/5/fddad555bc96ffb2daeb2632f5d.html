Winterset Madisonian – January 19, 1928, page 1

Death of Mrs. Lydia Hamilton
Mrs. Lydia Hamilton, who has been ill with pneumonia, for the past four weeks, died Wednesday morning, January 18th, between seven and eight o’clock, at the home of her daughter, Mrs. Billie Maxwell. Mrs. Hamilton was the second aged lady of Winterset, to die during the past week. She was 83 years of age.
Mrs. Hamilton, who was a daughter of Mr. and Mrs. John Freeman, was born in Elgin, Illinois, in 1844. Her mother died when she was a child and she was raised by her grandparents. When she was 22 years of age she was married to John Hamilton, and they came to Winterset to make their home. Mr. Hamilton died thirteen years ago and since that time Mrs. Hamilton has lived with the Maxwells.
Last summer, Mrs. Hamilton visited her daughter, Mrs. Pearl Niblo, in Huron, South Dakota, but shortly after her health began to fail, and she has not been well since then.
Funeral services will be conducted by Rev. F. Clare McCallon at the Church of Christ, Friday, January 20th, and burial will be made in the Winterset cemetery.
Mrs. Hamilton is survived by four children, Mrs. Edith Tilton, of Des Moines, Mrs. Pearl Niblo, of Huron, South Dakota, Hayes Hamilton, of Pitzer, and Mrs. Maxwell of Winterset.
