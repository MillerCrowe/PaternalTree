Note: 1 (Bio)

Mary Boardman (1739 – ca. 1804)
Mary Boardman was born on September 25, 1739, in Southington, Connecticut, the daughter of Ephraim Boardman and Mehitable Cole.
On January 14, 1759, Mary married Joab Hoisington in Farmington, Connecticut. Together, they had nine children, the first three born in Farmington or Southington and the remainder in Woodstock, Vermont, where the family settled in 1772. Joab, a prominent figure in early Vermont, became a tavern-keeper and a militia officer, with the family's home located near what is now the Old Constitution House in Windsor, Vermont.
Children of Mary and Joab Hoisington:
• Isaac (b. Nov 6, 1759, Farmington, VT)
• Bliss (b. 1762, Southington, CT – d. Feb 6, 1810, Massena, NY)
• Ozias (b. Nov 1763, Southington/Farmington, CT – d. 1812/15, NY)
• Verlina (b. Nov 1, 1765 – d. Apr 10, 1847, MI)
• Cynthia (b. 1768, Farmington, CT)
• Lavinia (b. 1770, Farmington, CT)
• Mary “Polly” (b. 1773, Farmington, CT)
• Barzava Zebulon (b. 1775, Farmington or Woodstock, CT – d. 1834, OH)
• Joab Jr. (b. Apr 28, 1777, Woodstock, VT – d. Jul 4, 1852, Athens, OH)
Joab passed away in 1777. Mary later became a member of Mr. Hutchinson’s Church in Woodstock and was referred to in records as the "widow Hoisington."
After Joab's death, Mary remarried, becoming the wife of Captain John Strong. With him, she moved to Argenteuil, on the banks of the Ottawa River in Lower Canada (now Quebec), around 1804.
Her family Bible, described as “Joab Hoisington’s grate Bible,” was a prized possession brought to Woodstock in 1772. After Joab’s death, Mary retained the Bible and took it to Canada, where it was ultimately destroyed in a house fire in 1840 at the home of a Mr. Burtch.
Mary Boardman died around 1804, in Argenteuil, Quebec. Her estate was executed on March 28, 1798, by her second husband John Strong, her son Ozias, and Benjamin Burtch. Her property, part of a 1000-acre parcel originally bought by Joab, was divided among her children.
